Voyager 1: Humanity's Farthest Explorer

The Interstellar Emissary

Voyager 1, launched on September 5, 1977, is a space probe that has become the first human-made object to enter interstellar space. It continues to be operational after over four decades, making it NASA's longest-running mission.

Key Facts:

  • Distance from Earth: Over 14 billion miles (as of 2023)
  • Speed: About 38,000 mph relative to the Sun
  • Power Source: Radioisotope Thermoelectric Generators (RTGs)
  • Main Objectives: Study the outer Solar System and interstellar medium

Voyager 1 carries a golden record containing sounds and images representing life and culture on Earth, serving as a time capsule for any extraterrestrial intelligence that might find it in the distant future.
